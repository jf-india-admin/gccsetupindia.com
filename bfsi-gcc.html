Who this is for

C-suite sponsors (CIO/COO/CTO/CISO) and GCC program leads at banks, insurers, and fintechs who need a pragmatic, compliance-first roadmap.

Captive vs BOT (at a glance)

Captive: full control, slower start, higher setup effort.

BOT: speed to market, contractual SLAs, planned transfer at 18–36 months.

12-Month Timeline (high level)

  • 0–4 wks: Business case, governance, program charter
  • 4–10 wks: Entity/tax, city shortlist, space plan, security baseline
  • 10–16 wks: Real estate & infra build, hiring leadership, policies
  • 16–28 wks: Pilot teams (engineering/ops/analytics), first audit
  • 28–52 wks: Scale to 150 FTE, stabilize KPIs, plan Phase-2

Compliance from day zero

FAQ

Do we need an Indian legal entity for a GCC?
Typically yes for a captive model. BOT can start under a partner with a planned transfer.
How do cross-border data flows work under DPDP?
Use data mapping, approved transfers, and access controls; see our Compliance Hub for patterns.
Typical time to break-even?
Often 12–18 months depending on ramp, real estate model, and baseline costs.
What functions go first (IT, analytics, ops, risk)?
Commonly IT/engineering and analytics first; shared services and risk follow once controls mature.
